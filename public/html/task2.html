Interview Analysis

Our team interviewed three undergraduate students from different degree programs at BUV to gather their feedback on our current LMS(Canvas). Our specific goal when approaching interviews was to understand students' experiences and perspectives on the merits and demerits of this platform. We aimed to empathize with our potential users' needs, behaviors, and motivations in order to develop LMS features that cater to their needs rather than just those of university administrators or stakeholders. The following are assessments to examine the present state and propose possible remedies to tackle the problems. The current application offers several benefits, including a user-friendly interface, organized and integrated courses, fast access to materials, and a basic information system that provides class/test schedules. These are key aspects that users should be aware of and consider when using the application. On the other hand, there are several features in the LMS that students find less valuable, such as Calendar, Studio, Discussion board, Files, and Groups. Most students rarely or never use these features except when necessary. The main reason for this trend is there are alternative tools with superior functions and easier accessibility, such as Google Calendar for Canvas Calendar, Outlook for Canvas Discussion, and Module for file sharing. Additionally, users continue to face difficulties in utilizing fundamental features of Canvas, such as the capability to promptly download study materials. To address the issues, several potential solutions have been identified, such as removing unnecessary features, simplifying logos and icons to highlight important features, adding optional retrieval/loading functionality for document groups, and integrating third-party tools like Outlook and MS Work. The interviews have presented different perspectives and solutions on the LMS, too. One interviewee shared that the Module section, which is the most interactive part of the LMS, sometimes has a lot of materials that make it difficult for students to distinguish between them just by the title. To resolve this, the interviewee suggested adding more information to the title, such as the date of each slide, or changing the title color for easier recognition. Another ideal solution was to provide a pop-up preview of the study materials when students hover over the link. A user has reported that students find it confusing to review their scores, as they are unsure whether to check the Assignment or the Grade sections. To address this issue and make it more straightforward for students, we should consider combining these two sections. For the notification system, a student expressed a desire for Canvas to provide an alert system that specifies the deadline of each assignment with a customizable date, such as "Ethical Hacking assignment deadline is in 10 days. Please review and complete your work for the best outcome!."
