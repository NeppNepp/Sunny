TASK 3_PERSONAS

Objective

This task has two objectives. The first objective is to create personas. The second objective of this task is to draft a Problem Statement.

“My lecturer posts a lot of resources on Canvas that really help me pursue my career. Despite that, I find Canvas really hard to navigate between pages to see the resources and the details on the resources is so scarce. Moreover, there are useless tools on Canvas and the interface of Canvas has too much information that makes it confusing to me.”

Demographic

Name: Tran Thi Thuy
Age: 19
Location: Ha Noi
Family: Not Living with parents
Occupation: University Student
Majoring: Marketing Management
Year: 1st year

Goal

• Be successful in her academic career: She is determined to excel in her studies and achieve academic success. She aims to gain knowledge, develop new skills, and perform well in her university courses.
• Make the most of the Learning Management System (LMS): She wants to fully utilize the university's LMS to access course materials, engage with her peers and instructors, and enhance her learning experience.

Pain Points

• Confusing navigation: She has trouble navigating the LMS interface. She prefers a user-friendly design that allows her to easily locate and access the desired content and features.
• Overwhelming wall of text: She prefers visual aids and concise information over excessive text. She finds it challenging to process large amounts of text and prefers visually engaging content.
• Useless features within modules: She feels frustrated when encountering unnecessary or redundant features within course modules. She desires a streamlined experience where every feature serves a clear purpose and enhances her learning.
• Lack of details/preview in hypertext content: She suggests that hypertext content should provide more details or previews to help her decide which content to explore. This would make navigation more efficient and allow her to make informed choices.

Problem Statement (Draft)

BUV's learning management system (Canvas) is deemed inefficient because of its faulty system for student education and growth.The existing system possesses four significant drawbacks that impede users from achieving their learning objectives: an inadequate interface, unnecessary features, an ineffective submission process, and an outdated communication system. There is no doubt that these challenges have considerable bearing on student involvement and inspiration, consequently leading to a poorer level of efficiency and academic achievement of BUV. The ideal LMS should aim to overcome these challenges to cater to the learning needs of students and create a new platform that can facilitate their educational and personal growth. The new UI should be designed with the user's needs in mind, including clear navigation and organization of content, visually appealing graphics, and customizable options, such as the ability to adjust font size, color scheme, and layout. In addition, the system should be able to support a variety of actions, including the implementation of a real-time messaging system for direct communication between instructors and students, the creation of discussion forums for collaborative learning and problem-solving, and the provision of notifications for new messages and replies. User testing should be conducted annually to gather feedback and identify pain points. Therefore, we can regularly eliminate unnecessary features and maintain the interface to ensure optimal performance.
