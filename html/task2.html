TASK 2 INTERVIEW

Objective

The objective of this interview is to understand usage behaviors of students and identify major issues that they have encountered since using the Learning Management System. These issues can be related to design, navigation, functionality, and experience that creates inconvenience or discomfort while using the LMS. The information gathered in this interview will be analyzed in order to better understand usersâ€™ needs. This leads to more appropriate product development strategies.

Target Audience

This interview target students in British University of Vietnam.
The majority of users using the mentioned LMS are students.

Interview Guide

- How usually do you use the LMS?
- What do you use the LMS for?
- What functions do you use the most?
- What functions do you use the least?
- Do you have any difficulties when navigating in the page?
- How do you rate the design from 1 to 10?
- Have you encountered any technical issues or glitches while using the LMS?
- Are there any features or functions that you feel are missing or could be improved in the LMS?
- How effective do you find the LMS in meeting your learning or training needs?
In your opinion, how user-friendly is the LMS for both administrators and learners?

Analysis_Interview

Canvas provides several advanced capabilities of an LMS, including diverse learning material formats, such as video, and files, as well as the option to employ quizzes as either assessments or learning materials. Furthermore, Canvas offers web conferencing, complete with screen-sharing, recording, and replay features. However, students have access to learning resources for their registered courses for only one semester. Functions such as discussion boards and online conferences necessitate authorization for access. This LMS should offer unlimited access to all the learning materials for the courses they are registered for, as well as the ability to send messages to any user on the platform. Hosting online conferences should not require permission from an administrator and should be accessible to every user with the support of document-sharing and third-party tools.

In contrast, Duolingo does not allow users to upload files. Instead, it provides a collection of learning materials, including mini-games and interactive/audio lessons, and guild mechanics to facilitate self-learning. Additionally, Duolingo lacks support for online conferences and discussion forums. Some suggestions for improving this site include improving communication between students and teachers and giving teachers more flexibility in structuring their sessions.

Text-based resources, videos, audio, quizzes, and other learning tools are all supported by TalentLMS. The platform is designed to allow easy sharing of course structures within a group and is accessible for students to review past courses. TalentLMS features a user-friendly discussion area and supports various learning methods (peer-to-peer learning, collaborative projects). However, as the complexity and number of users expand, the platform's performance may suffer. Besides providing high-quality virtual classrooms with beneficial functions, namely: scheduling, taking notes, and recording, what sets TalentLMS apart from other LMSs is its ability to incorporate third-party tools. This feature allows for the creation of separate discussion areas for each mini-course, which can help enhance users' concentration. In order to improve the user participation experience of TalentLMS, its discussion area needs to be able to adapt and handle bigger volumes and more complicated discussions.
